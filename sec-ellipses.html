    ðŸ”—

    Subsection 4.4.2 Equation of an Ellipse

    In order to obtain the simplest equation for an ellipse, we place the ellipse at standard position. An ellipse is said to be at standard position when its center is at the origin and its major axis lies on either the x-axis or y-axis.
    ðŸ”—

    Subsubsection 4.4.2.1 Equation of an ellipse at standard position

    There are two possible situations, namely, when the major axis lies on x-axis (called horizontal ellipse) and when the major axis lies on y-axis (called vertical ellipse). We first consider a horizontal ellipse as in Figure 4.19
    ðŸ”—
    Figure 4.19: Horizontal ellipse at standard position
    ðŸ”—
    Let the center of the ellipse be at the origin, C(0,0) and foci at Fâ€²(âˆ’c,0), F (c,0) and vertices at (âˆ’a,0) and (a,0) (see Figure 4.19). Then, a point P(x,y) is on the ellipse iff
    ðŸ”—
    |PFâ€²| + |PF| = 2a .
    ðŸ”—
    That is, \(\sqrt{(x+c)^2+ y^2}+ \sqrt{(x-c)^2 + y^2} = 2a \)
    ðŸ”—
    or \(\sqrt{(x-c)^2 + y^2} = 2a - \sqrt{(x+c)^2 + y^2}\)
    ðŸ”—
    Squaring both sides we get
    ðŸ”—
    \(x^2 - 2cx + c^2 + y^2 = 4a^2 - 4a\sqrt{(x+c)^2 + y^2} + x^2 + 2cx+ c^2 + y^2\)
    ðŸ”—
    which simplifies to \(a\sqrt{(x+c)^2 + y^2} = a^2 + cx\)
    ðŸ”—
    Again squaring both sides, we get \(a^2(x^2+2cx+c^2+y^2) a^4 + 2a^2cx+c^2x^2\)
    ðŸ”—
    Which becomes \((a^2-c^2)x^2 + a^2y^2 = a^2(a^2-c^2)\)
    ðŸ”—
    Now recalling that \(b^2 = a^2 - c^2\) and dividing both sides by \(a^2b^2\text{,}\) the equation becomes
    ðŸ”—
    \(\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\) (Equation of horizontal ellipse at standard position, vertices (Â±a, 0), foci (Â±c, 0), where \(c^2 = a^2 - b^2\))
    For a vertical ellipse at standard position, the same procedure gives the equation
    ðŸ”—
    \(\frac{x^2}{b^2} + \frac{y^2}{a^2} = 1\) (Equation of vertical ellipse at standard position, vertices (0,Â±a), foci (0,Â±c), where \(c^2 = a^2 - b^2\))
